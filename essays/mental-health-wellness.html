Why Mental Health Is Not Optional Anymore

July 2025

Living and learning in today’s world demands constant output. For aspirants and working individuals alike, days often turn into an endless cycle of tasks, deadlines, and expectations.

When our thoughts are constantly racing, we fail to prioritize recovery. Over time, this leads to cognitive fatigue—a condition recognized in neuroscience and psychology.

Deadlines are classic triggers for cognitive overload. While some experience this early in life, others carry it silently for years.

Adequate rest, creative hobbies, journaling, proper nutrition, self-care, and time in nature are not luxuries. They are maintenance.

Mental health is not about slowing life down. It is about sustaining it.
