Library

A curated collection of books that shaped my thinking, emotions, discipline, and understanding of human nature.

Spiritual & Philosophical Foundations

Shrimad Bhagavad Gita

A timeless guide on duty, detachment, and clarity. Each reading reveals a new layer of meaning.

The Forty Rules of Love — Elif Shafak

A gentle yet powerful exploration of love, surrender, and spiritual transformation.

The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari — Robin Sharma

A simple reminder that inner wealth always matters more than outward success.

Fiction & Storytelling

The Alchemist — Paulo Coelho

A quiet, reflective story about faith, timing, and listening to oneself.

A Little Life — Hanya Yanagihara

Emotionally intense and unforgettable. A story that stays long after the last page.

Eleven Minutes — Paulo Coelho

A reflective narrative on desire, identity, and emotional vulnerability.

Fairy Tales

Stories that remind me why imagination and symbolism matter at every age.

Mythology & Reimagined History

The Shiva Trilogy — Amish Tripathi

(The Immortals of Meluha · The Secret of the Nagas · The Oath of the Vayuputras)
A modern retelling that blends philosophy, leadership, and myth.

The Hidden Hindu

A fast-paced blend of mythology and mystery with spiritual undertones.

Mystery & Suspense

A Good Girl’s Guide to Murder Series — Holly Jackson

(A Good Girl’s Guide to Murder · Good Girl, Bad Blood · As Good As Dead)
Sharp, engaging, and addictive. A reminder that storytelling can be both smart and gripping.

Habits, Discipline & Self-Growth

Atomic Habits — James Clear

Practical, realistic, and effective. A book I return to when consistency matters more than motivation.

The Ultimate Gift — Jim Stovall

A short but meaningful reminder about values, gratitude, and responsibility.
